# Event: MEET 13/06/2026

**Date & Time:** 09:00 - 12:00, June 13, 2026  
**Location:** 26th Floor, Bitexco Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Saigon Ward, Ho Chi Minh City  
**Role:** Attendee  

The MEET 13/06/2026 event is a networking and practical sharing session from IT industry experts. Below is a summary of the insights gained from the 4 key speakers:

### 1. From Practical Stories to Culture at a Multinational Corporation & Mindset for 3rd-4th Year Students Amidst Recruitment Fluctuations
**Speaker:** Pham Tien Dat (Mr. Dat Pham - Data Analytics Engineer) & Nguyen Hung Cuong (Mr. Cuong Nguyen - Process Engineer)

- **Work Reality:** Analyzed the Data Analytics Engineer role in different environments such as Kamereo (building operational reports, designing Dashboards, analyzing business metrics) and Colgate-Palmolive (machinery data projects, IoT devices, production cost optimization).
- **Required Skills & Mindset:** Emphasized the importance of critical thinking, communication skills, problem-solving, and data storytelling. Shared a career development model through stages: Follower, Learner, Problem Solver, System Thinker, and Super Star.
- **Corporate Culture at MNCs:** Decoded the standard recruitment process (Screening, Competency Test, Technical Interview using the STAR model, Cultural Fit). Introduced distinct cultural traits like No-Blame Post-Mortem (focusing on root causes instead of blaming) and Caring & Inclusive environments. Also touched upon lessons from East Asian countries (Japan, South Korea) and Vietnam's digital history flow.

### 2. A scalable URL shortening service on AWS
**Speaker:** Dinh Trung Kien & Nguyen Minh Tho

- **Content Summary:** An introduction to building a scalable URL shortening service with a high-load architecture based on Amazon Web Services (AWS) cloud services.

### 3. From First Cloud AI Journey to AWS Partner
**Speaker:** Danh Hoang Hieu Nghi (AI Engineer – AWS Community Builder – AWS Student Builder Group Leader)

- **Content Summary:**
  - Shared a journey of personal and career development through AWS community programs, including: First Cloud AI Journey Program (formerly First Cloud Journey), AWS Student Builder Group Program (formerly AWS Cloud Clubs), and the AWS Community Builder Program.
  - Oriented future technical roles such as Solutions Architect, DevOps Engineer, Platform Engineer, and Software Engineer.
  - Highlighted transition milestones and networking aimed at becoming an AWS Partner.

### 4. What does a DevOps Engineer really do?
**Speaker:** Truong H. Trong (Trong H. Truong - DevOps Engineer @ Endava Vietnam)

- **Career Reality:** Distinguished between theoretical definitions/misconceptions (just writing CI/CD, doing Docker/K8s, fixing bugs at midnight) and the actual day-to-day work of a DevOps Engineer.
- **Learning Path:** Advised learners to focus on core fundamentals first, such as Linux, basic Networking, programming languages (Python, Golang), Git, CI/CD, and Containers. Encouraged understanding how applications operate and building small hands-on projects.
- **Practical Lessons:** Shared valuable lessons learned from experience: understanding concepts rather than just copying commands, correctly identifying the subject of a problem, always asking "why" before "how", and recognizing that communication is a crucial part of the job.
- **Qualities of a Great DevOps:** Having a system mindset rather than just completing fragmented tasks, automating boring repetitive work, simplifying everything for the team, and knowing how to leverage AI to boost skills rather than abusing it.

### Event Experience

The "MEET 13/06/2026" event was highly oriented towards networking and career development, giving me a much more realistic and profound view of the IT job market. The standout values I learned include:

- **Understanding Career Realities:** Listening to expert analyses helped me dispel misconceptions about the roles of a DevOps Engineer or Data Analytics Engineer, thereby guiding me to focus on the truly important core fundamentals.
- **Corporate Culture Awareness:** Grasping the standard recruitment process and distinct cultural traits (like No-Blame Post-Mortem) at multinational corporations (MNCs) helped me better prepare mentally and refine soft skills amidst recruitment fluctuations.
- **System Architecture Mindset:** The analysis of an AWS URL shortening service provided a typical, practical example of how to design a high-load and scalable architecture.
- **Motivation for Community Development:** The journey from being an AWS Cloud Clubs student member to becoming an AWS Partner was deeply inspiring, encouraging me to engage more actively in tech communities to expand my network and elevate my career.
